# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
Designs intuitive, user-centered interfaces for all product experiences.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing to inform design decisions
- Translate requirements into wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Developers to ensure technical feasibility
- Define and document UX best practices and design standards for the team

### Goals
- Deliver user-centered designs that improve usability and satisfaction
- Ensure consistent design principles across all product experiences
- Align the team on design intent and reduce rework

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and usability testing sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Retrospectives and feedback sessions to iterate on design quality
- Design system documentation and prototype handoffs

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Translates data into actionable insights to inform product and project decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Analyze usage, performance, and customer data to surface trends and opportunities
- Develop dashboards and communicate findings to stakeholders
- Support Product Managers in establishing and tracking success metrics
- Identify data quality issues and propose improvements

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making across the team
- Provide measurable evidence of product and project impact
- Improve the reliability and accessibility of data insights

### Typical Communication
- Analytics reviews with Product and Project Managers
- Data findings shared in planning and retrospective sessions
- Dashboard and report documentation for stakeholders

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
Manages infrastructure, CI/CD, and operational reliability for streamlined delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Automate deployment and testing pipelines to reduce manual effort
- Monitor application health and resolve operational incidents
- Ensure best practices in security, performance, and scalability
- Maintain documentation on operational flows and runbooks

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, repeatable deployments with minimal friction
- Reduce mean time to recovery and improve system observability
- Uphold operational excellence across the delivery lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Pipeline and release coordination with Developers and QA/Testers
- Operational risk advisories and runbook reviews with Project Managers
- Incident retrospectives and post-mortems

---

## How These Personas Interact

The table below summarizes the primary collaboration points between roles:

| Role | Works Closely With | Key Touchpoints |
|---|---|---|
| Developer | Product Manager, Project Manager, UX Designer, DevOps Engineer | Sprint planning, code reviews, design handoffs, pipeline automation |
| Product Manager | Developer, Project Manager, UX Designer, Data Analyst | Roadmap alignment, success metrics, usability validation |
| Project Manager | Developer, Product Manager, DevOps Engineer, Data Analyst | Status reporting, risk management, retrospectives, planning |
| UX Designer | Product Manager, Developer | Requirements translation, usability testing, design reviews |
| Data Analyst | Product Manager, Project Manager, Developer | Metrics definition, analytics reviews, data source validation |
| DevOps Engineer | Developer, Project Manager, QA/Tester | Release coordination, operational risk advisories, incident response |

---

## When to Involve Each Role

| Project Phase | Roles to Involve |
|---|---|
| Initiation & Planning | Product Manager, Project Manager, UX Designer, Data Analyst |
| Design & Discovery | UX Designer, Product Manager, Developer |
| Development | Developer, UX Designer, DevOps Engineer |
| Testing & QA | Developer, DevOps Engineer |
| Release & Deployment | DevOps Engineer, Developer, Project Manager |
| Post-Release & Retrospective | All roles — Product Manager, Project Manager, Developer, UX Designer, Data Analyst, DevOps Engineer |

---

## Future Personas to Consider

As projects scale, the following roles may warrant formal definition in future documentation updates:

- **QA Automation Engineer** – Owns automated test strategy and tooling to complement Developer and DevOps workflows
- **Customer Support Specialist** – Bridges customer feedback with Product and Engineering to surface recurring issues and improvement opportunities
- **Security Engineer** – Embeds security best practices into the development lifecycle, advises DevOps on compliance and threat modeling
- **Technical Writer** – Produces and maintains end-user and internal documentation, partnering with all technical roles
